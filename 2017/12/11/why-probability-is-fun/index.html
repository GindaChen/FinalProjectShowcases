Why Probability Is Fun?

发表于 2017-12-11

We often felt that there is not less, and perhaps even more, beauty in the result of analysis than there is to be found in mere contemplation.

— Niko Tinbergen, Curious Naturalists (1958).

Now I post a very interesting, intriguing question in the book, 40 Puzzles and Problems in Mathematical Statistics by Wolfgang Schwarz, one of the famous probablist in the world ( to test how \(\LaTeX\) works ):

1.16 Is \(2X\) the same as \(X_1 + X_2\) ?

Let \(X\) be a continuous random variabe (rv) with density \(f\), and let \(X_1\), \(X_2\) be two independent rvs, both distributed as is \(X\). It is then not usually the case that the rv \(2X\) is distributed as is \(X_1 + X_2\). However, the Cauchy density whose standardized form is given by \[ f(x) = \frac1{\pi} \dot{} \frac1{1+x^2} \] possesses this property: \(X_1 + X_2\) has the same distribution as the rv \(2X\). It is ilustrated and compared to the standard normal distribution in Figure 1.1 (which is to be generated later).

​ a. Based on the property described earlier, argue without any explicit calculation that the variance of the Cauchy distribution is necessarily infinite.

​ b. Give an inductive argument for the rather unintuitive feature that for the Cauchy distribution the arithemetic mean from a sample of \(n=2^k\) independent realizations of \(X\) has exactly the same distribution as each contributing summand itself.
