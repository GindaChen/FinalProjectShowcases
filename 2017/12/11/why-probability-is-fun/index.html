1.16 Is \(2X\) the same as \(X_1 + X_2\) ?

Let \(X\) be a continuous random variabe (rv) with density \(f\), and let \(X_1\), \(X_2\) be two independent rvs, both distributed as is \(X\). It is then not usually the case that the rv \(2X\) is distributed as is \(X_1 + X_2\). However, the Cauchy density whose standardized form is given by \[ f(x) = \frac1{\pi} \dot{} \frac1{1+x^2} \] possesses this property: \(X_1 + X_2\) has the same distribution as the rv \(2X\).
